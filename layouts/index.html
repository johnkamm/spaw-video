Cyrus McCormick revolutionized agriculture

That's not controversial. He marketed a machine that harvested grain, a crop that was harvested in years prior . . . by hand. By hand.

As improved and specialized machines were applied to agriculture, production costs dropped, often by orders of magnitude. McCormick started a revolution, and now cash crops of every stripe are planted, tended and harvested by bespoke machines-- from berries to root vegetables to tree fruit.

Is farming row-crop cannabis mechanized in the same way? It's not. Outdoor THC cannabis plantations largely rely on human labor for each step in that crop's cultivation. It's a predictably expensive process.

We're the exception: our team has engineered technology that halves the OpEx top line that rules the industry. We've changed how outdoor cannabis is cultivated by using mechanical advantages at every step of cultivation-- germination, planting, fertigation, trellising, harvesting.

We get it done cheaper, we get it done faster.

Fewer hands, more horsepower.
